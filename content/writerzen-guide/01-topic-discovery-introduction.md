# 1.1 Introduction to Topic Discovery

## Overview

This lesson provides an overview of Topic Discovery 2, including how it works, what it shows, and how to use it. The next lesson covers a deeper dive into the results.

## Purpose of Topic Discovery

The main purpose of Topic Discovery 2 is to assist with the research phase of your content creation process. It enables you to get a solid grasp of your market in just a few minutes.

Key things you can do with this tool:

- Understand your target audience's needs and interests
- Research your competitors' actions in the market
- Quickly spot opportunities such as new surging ideas or emerging needs to target
- Come up with a list of topics for your content plan based on your chosen keywords

Understanding your target audience is essential for ranking in Google today. The more you understand their pain points and needs, the more you can address those issues with solutions. More topic coverage builds your topical authority and signals to Google that you are an expert on the subject. Google evaluates content based on E-E-A-T: Experience, Expertise, Authority, and Trust.

## How It Works (Mechanism & Data Source)

When you enter a seed keyword, WriterZen searches the top 100 results that Google returns for that keyword. It then scans the entire content of all 100 competitor pages and filters out all headlines and sub-headlines from those results to determine how many competitors are covering certain topics and subtopics.

From this data, the tool presents your competitive performance data grouped into topic cards.

Additional data surfaced by the tool includes:

- Headlines to consider
- Related Google searches
- Google Suggest insights
- Search intent data derived from user behaviour

## Creating a New Search

You can access Topic Discovery from the navigation menu on your dashboard, or directly via the Topic Discovery card which shows a list of all previous projects.

To start a new project:

1. Click the link to create a new project
2. In the main dashboard, type any keyword into the search bar
3. Choose the language and location for the project (you can save these as defaults if your target market is consistent)
4. Click Search — your research will be saved and accessible from the history table

If your plan includes the Team Function, an additional field for the owner of the topic research will be visible.

> **Note:** Search results expire after 30 days. Google's results are highly fluctuating at the 30-day mark. If there is a gap between when you conducted the research and when you create your content, start a fresh search to ensure accuracy.

## Example

To research a topic, enter the seed keyword, set location and language if different from the default, and click Search.

For example, searching "nutrition plan" returns a list of 42 topics when relevancy is set to Medium. These represent the topics needed to build credibility and demonstrate expertise in that subject.
