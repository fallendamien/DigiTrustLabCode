# Introduction to Keyword Explorer

## Overview

The Keyword Explorer tool is designed with three main goals: to show how your chosen seed keyword compares to other relevant popular search results, to help you quickly build keyword lists for different stages of your SEO campaign using basic and advanced filters, and to reduce the average time spent on manual keyword research by up to 90%.

## Popular Use Cases

Some of the most common use cases for Keyword Explorer include finding suitable golden keywords for your seed keyword or topic, understanding the popularity of your seed keyword within a particular demographic, gauging the competition for your seed keyword by looking at the leading articles in the SERPs, and helping you fully optimize your content for the SERPs. The tool streamlines the process of finding, clustering, and optimizing keywords for your content, while also providing deeper insight through multiple metrics and data points.

## How It Works

When you type a seed keyword into Keyword Explorer — whether it's a keyword search, domain search, or wildcard search — WriterZen uses Google's own API to search through the highest-ranked results from Google's keyword database and Google Suggest. This surfaces keyword clusters most relevant to your seed keyword.

You're then presented with all the data needed to evaluate your keyword, including search metrics, trends, and domains, along with all related keywords worth considering. From there, you can use the various filters to sort the data according to your goal and save your selections to keyword lists within WriterZen.

## Using the Interface

To create a new search, access the Keyword Explorer tool via the navigation bar or through the card on the dashboard by clicking the "Explore New Keyword" link. Inside the dashboard, type your seed keyword into the search bar, then choose a language and location for each individual search or project. To save time, you can set a default language and location if your target market is generally consistent.

Your latest projects are accessible directly from the Keyword Explorer card on the main dashboard. Your full search history is available in the history table inside the Keyword Explorer dashboard, where you'll find details including the keyword, location, language, date created, and status. If your plan includes the team function, you'll also see a column indicating the owner of each research entry.

It's worth noting that search results expire after 30 days. This is to protect you from working with outdated information, as Google's data is highly fluctuating — especially around the 30-day mark. If there's a gap between when you conducted your research and when you work with the data, the safest approach is to start fresh.
