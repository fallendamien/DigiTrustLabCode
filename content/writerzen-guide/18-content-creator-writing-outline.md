# 4.1 Content Creator: Writing Outline

## Overview

This lesson covers the layout of the Content Creator tool and how to build your article outline — including the research panel, competitive analysis, and outline building functions.

## Interface Layout

The Content Creator screen is split into two sides:

| Side | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Left panel** | Research area — all data, insights, and reference material |
| **Right panel** | Outline panel — where you build and structure your article |

---

## Right Panel: Outline Builder

### Step 1 — Set General Article Info

**Title**
- Type your title manually, or click the AI assistant button to generate title suggestions based on your seed keyword
- Select a title you like and click Add to confirm

**Description**
- Once a title is set, the AI assistant button for description becomes active
- Click Generate to create a description based on your title
- Select and add the one you prefer

**Content Structure**
- WriterZen automatically suggests a minimum word count based on competitor benchmarks
- Scroll the word count slider to see how your length compares to competitors
- Recommended position: above 70–80% of competitors (typically ~1,000–1,300 words for a blog post)
- The number of **headings** scales automatically with word count but can be manually adjusted
- **Paragraphs** and **images** can also be set manually
- Click **Done** then **Save** to confirm your structure

> **Tip:** On smaller screens, click the scroll-up button in the top-right corner of the outline panel to maximise building space. On larger screens, leave the title/description visible for reference.

### Building the Outline

Once structure is saved, the outline panel opens for development. You can:

- **Double-click** any heading from competitor summaries to add it to your outline
- Items appear in the format they were in the source (H1 → H2 in your outline since H1 is already set, bullet points stay as bullet points)
- **Multi-select** items using Shift (Windows: Ctrl) to bulk-format or move them
- **Change heading level** — convert any item to H2, H3, H4, or bullet point
- **Move items** — drag and reorder individual or grouped outline items
- **Add new line** — hover over any heading and click Add Outline to insert a new item below it; double-click to type your own heading
- **Edit** any item — double-click to open the edit box, make changes, click Yes to confirm
- **Delete** items individually or via multi-select + Delete
- **Remove All** — clears the entire outline (requires confirmation)

> **Blue line indicator:** When clicking items from the research panel, a blue highlight line shows exactly where the new item will be inserted in your outline.

---

## Left Panel: Research Area

### Competitive Analysis
Shows data from the **top 20 competitors** for your seed keyword, including:
- Percentage of first-page results that are articles (vs. ecommerce pages)
- The higher this percentage, the better an article will perform for this keyword
- Click any competitor to open a **Summary View** — an AI-generated outline of that competitor's content structure (H1, H2, headings, paragraphs, and FAQ view)
- From the summary view, click or double-click any heading to add it to your outline

> You can also access the top 20 competitors directly from within the competitor summary panel — no need to navigate back.

### Google Suggests Insights
- **People Also Ask** — questions pulled from Google's PAA panel and Google Suggest database
- **Topics** — related ideas and discussions people are exploring
- **Comparisons** — things people are comparing in this niche
- **Reddit Topics** — questions being asked on Reddit related to your keyword

Click any question or topic to add it directly to your outline at the currently selected position.

### AI Shortcuts (Outline Items)
When hovering over any outline item, AI shortcuts appear:
- **Expand** — expand the heading into a paragraph
- **Rewrite** — rewrite the item
- **Topic paragraph** — generate a paragraph around that topic
- **Copy** — copy the item

> AI assistant features are covered in detail in the next lesson.

---

## Key Takeaway

The outline step is the foundation of your content. The left panel gives you everything you need for research (competitors, questions, topics), while the right panel gives you a fluid drag-and-drop environment to build a structured, competitive outline before writing a single word.
