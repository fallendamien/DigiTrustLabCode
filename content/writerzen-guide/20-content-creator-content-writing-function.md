# 4.3 Content Creator: Content Writing Function

## Overview

Step 3 is where you execute your content — writing the article based on the outline and keywords prepared in Steps 1 and 2. Everything in this step is designed to keep you focused purely on writing.

## Interface Layout

The screen is divided into:
- **Top right corner** — scoring and analysis panel
- **Right side panels** — research data from previous steps (outline, keywords, competitors, questions)
- **Bottom panel** — AI assistant, save controls, and article info
- **Left panel** — article information and sharing tools
- **Centre** — the writing editor

---

## Top Right: Overall Score & Analysis

| Element | What It Shows |
|---|---|
| **Content Score** | Live score measuring how well-optimised your article is as you write |
| **Word count** | Tracks against the target you set in Step 1 |
| **Headings / Paragraphs / Images** | Progress against the targets you configured in Step 1 |
| **Show Analysis button** | Opens the analysis panel — scans your content and flags SEO issues (e.g. missing internal/external links, heading structure, writing improvements) |

> **Recommendation:** Aim for more than 3 internal and external links per post.

---

## Right Side: Research Data Panels

### 1. Outline
Click **Outline** to open your Step 1 outline in the left panel. Use it as a reference while writing, or click **Copy to Editor** to paste the full outline structure directly into the writing area as a starting scaffold.

### 2. Keywords
Displays all keywords selected in Step 2, with their target usage count (e.g. "2/11" means used 2 times, target is 11).

- Toggle **Highlight Keyword** to see your keywords highlighted in the editor in real time as you write
- Usage count updates live as you type
- Click any keyword to see examples of how competitors used it in their content — click through to view the actual competitor page

### 3. Competitors
Shows all top 20 competitors ordered by ranking, with word count and (if Google NLP is enabled) sentiment data.

- Click any competitor to open their **Summary View** (AI-generated outline of their content)
- Click **FAQ Panel** to see AI-generated FAQs derived from that competitor's article
- For competitors without an FAQ format, click **Generate FAQ** to create one on demand
- Click **Next** to cycle through all 20 competitors

### 4. Questions
A compiled list of People Also Ask questions, Google Suggest queries, and Reddit topics related to your keyword.

> This panel is designed as a **quick reference while writing** — not a research tool. The heavy research was done in Steps 1 and 2. Use this to quickly look up questions or angles as you write.

---

## Bottom Panel: Save & AI Controls

| Button | Function |
|---|---|
| **AI Assistant** | Opens the AI assistant panel (covered in detail in dedicated AI lessons) |
| **Auto Save** | Content saves automatically every ~10 seconds — no action needed |
| **Save** | Manual save — use when you want to exit immediately and ensure nothing is lost. Status shows as "In Progress" on the project dashboard |
| **Done** | Marks the article as finished. Status changes to "Finished" on the project management dashboard |

> **Key difference — Save vs Done:** Save = still in progress, you will return. Done = article is complete and marked finished in your project.

---

## Left Panel: Article Info & Sharing

| Feature | Function |
|---|---|
| **Deadline** | Displays the deadline set when the article was created |
| **Import Content** | Import existing content from a URL or file directly into the editor |
| **Note** | View notes left for writers when the article was created — useful for briefing freelancers |
| **Share** | Generate a shareable link for writers. Set permissions for what they can access: competitor panel, related questions, plagiarism checker |

> **Note on AI access:** Writers with shared access **cannot** use the AI assistant. Only the account owner can use AI in content creation. This is a planned Team Function feature for future development.

---

## Writing Editor

The editor toolbar includes standard formatting controls (bold, italic, table, image upload, indent, undo/redo) plus:

- **Inline text toolbar** — highlight any text to access quick formatting and AI shortcuts
- **Right-click menu** — opens the AI shortcut panel using either:
  - **Highlighted text** as the AI input (when text is selected)
  - **All text above the cursor** as the AI input (when nothing is selected)

---

## Plagiarism Checker (End of Writing)

Once your article is complete, run a plagiarism check from the right panel:

- Click **Quick View Report** for a summary inline
- Click **Detail Report** to go to the full Plagiarism Checker tool

WriterZen's plagiarism checker uses Google's web index database — it checks only against **indexed sources**, since these are the ones that can affect your ranking ability. Non-indexed PDFs or documents may still appear in results.

---

## Key Takeaway

By the time you reach Step 3, the hard work is done. Your outline is set, your keywords are chosen. Step 3 is purely about writing — everything in the interface is designed to support that focus without distraction.
