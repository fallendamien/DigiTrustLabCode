# 2.2 How to Use Keyword Search in Keyword Explorer

## Overview

This lesson covers the Keyword Search functionality in Keyword Explorer — how to gather keyword variations, understand the metrics, and use filters and tabs to find the best keywords for your content plan.

## What Keyword Search Does

Keyword Search lets you quickly gather all variations of your seed keyword. This often surfaces better keywords to target than the seed keyword itself.

The data comes directly from Google's own API — not an in-house algorithm — making it the most accurate data available. It includes essential metrics such as:

- CPC (Cost Per Click)
- Keyword Difficulty
- Search Volume
- Competition

Keyword Search also includes a range of basic and advanced filters to help you shape the data, and lets you save keyword lists to access later when creating content.

## Creating a New Search

The search bar contains three tabs: **Keyword**, **Domain**, and **Wildcard**. Keyword Search is the default tab.

Additional settings next to the search bar:
- **Language dropdown** — choose from over 45 languages
- **Location dropdown** — set your target location

You can save your preferred language and location as the default if your target market is consistent, saving time on future searches.

To run a search, enter any seed keyword into the search box and click Search.

> **Tip:** Enable Textbook Mode in the results screen to see tooltips when hovering over underlined elements. Detailed explanations and use cases are also available in the Learning Hub (top right of screen).

## Top Right Panels

### SERPs Overview
Displays the top-ranking results for your seed keyword. Use these competitor articles as a reference when creating content with a good chance of ranking.

### Overview Chart
Shows search trends for your seed keyword, helping you quickly gauge whether the keyword is seasonal or on an upward or downward trend.

- **Search Volume tab** — shows the average monthly search volume
- **Trend tab** — shows the frequency of the search term relative to its total search volume over time, on a scale of 1 to 100. Data sourced directly from Google Trends.

## Top Left Panels

Four metric cards provide a snapshot of your seed keyword:

| Card | What It Shows |
|---|---|
| **Search Volume** | Last month's search count, 12-month average, highest and lowest peaks |
| **CPC** | Average cost per click — what advertisers pay each time someone clicks an ad targeting this keyword |
| **Keyword Ideas** | Number of keyword ideas generated using Google's keyword database and Google Keyword Suggest |
| **Total Search Volume** | Combined search volume across all relevant keywords |

## Keyword Data Tab

The Keyword Data tab shows a complete list of keywords relevant to your seed keyword, organised into four sub-tabs:

| Sub-tab | What It Shows |
|---|---|
| **Total** | All variations of your seed keyword with their related metrics |
| **Phrase Match** | Keyword ideas that include your keyword in the exact order written |
| **Having Same Terms** | Keyword phrases that include your keyword in any order |
| **Also Search For** | Related keywords that users have searched for alongside your keyword |

Below the sub-tabs, a set of basic and advanced filters lets you narrow and shape your keyword lists within any of the four tabs.

Click any keyword inside the Keyword Data tab to expand it and see detailed metrics for that specific keyword.

## Insights Tab

The Insights tab shows content ideas grouped into clusters of closely related search queries. Use this to find what questions people are asking and plan content that provides helpful answers.

Categories include:

**Questions** — Frequently asked questions Google shows in SERPs, grouped by question type (what, where, when, why, who, how, etc.). Expand each card to explore results in detail. A high CPC on a question keyword is a strong signal that there is money in that market.

**Prepositions** — Results containing at least one preposition (with, for, without, etc.).

**Comparisons** — Results containing comparative words. Review these cards carefully; some may be brand-related with low value.

**Alphabet** — Results containing a single letter. These can surface grey keywords with steady search volume worth exploring.

**Numerical** — Results containing a number. Often reveals strong specific keywords such as "1000 calorie diet," "one week diet," or "one month diet plan."
