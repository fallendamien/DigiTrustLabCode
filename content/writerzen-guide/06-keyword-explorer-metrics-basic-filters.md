# 2.4 Keyword Metrics and Basic Filter Set

## Overview

This lesson covers the key metrics and basic filters available in Keyword Explorer, using "nutrition plan" as the sample case. Advanced filters — Golden Filter, Keyword Clustering, and Revenue Forecast — are covered in separate lessons.

## Keyword Metrics

Enable Textbook Mode at any time to see definitions of each metric on screen.

### Search Volume
The Volume column shows the monthly average number of searches for a keyword. Search volumes fluctuate month to month — similar to trend data — so results may vary depending on when you search.

Click any keyword to expand it and see 12 months of trend data via the chart below.

### Trend
The trend chart shows monthly search volume for each keyword over the last 12 months, giving you a quick visual of whether interest is growing, declining, or stable.

### CPC (Cost Per Click)
Shows the average cost per click for your keyword — how much advertisers pay each time someone clicks an ad targeting that keyword.

### PPC Competition
Shows how many competitors are bidding on each keyword. A useful indicator of keyword profitability.

### Keyword Difficulty (KD)
Accessible by clicking a keyword to expand its detailed metrics. KD measures your chances of ranking in the top 10 of the SERPs.

Difficulty is determined by:
- Amount of content written about that keyword
- Level of competition for Google Ads
- Number of brand mentions
- Amount of traffic for that keyword

## Basic Filters

Filters let you narrow down your keyword data quickly — saving significant time compared to doing this manually.

### Include Filter
Shows only results that contain words you specify. Two modes:

| Mode | Behaviour |
|---|---|
| **All Words** | Results must contain every word you specify |
| **Any Words** | Results can contain any one of the words you specify |

**Example:** Typing "plan" and "muscle" into All Words returns only phrases containing both words (91 results, ~23,000 combined monthly volume). Switching to Any Words returns phrases containing either word (2,000+ results, ~907,000 combined monthly volume).

### Exclude Filter
Removes results that contain unwanted words — useful for cleaning up irrelevant keywords from your list.

### Volume, CPC, and Word Count Filters
Set minimum and maximum values for each metric. The Word Count filter is particularly useful when you need to filter out long-tail keywords.

### Keyword Trending Filter
Shows only keywords that display a star icon in the Trending column, indicating a recent upward search trend.

## Advanced Filters (Overview)

These are covered in detail in dedicated lessons, but here is a brief overview:

| Filter | What It Does |
|---|---|
| **Golden Filter** | Calculates a keyword's Golden Score — its probability to rank or potential long-term SEO value. The lower the score, the more likely it is to rank. |
| **Revenue Forecast** | Generates a revenue forecast for your target keyword list based on your conversion rate, CTR, revenue per conversion, and expected revenue. |
| **Cluster** | Organises your keywords into groups based on how many similar results rank on the first page of Google in each cluster. Uses Google's own API for 100% accurate clustering. |

## Supporting Functions

### Add To
Adds selected keywords to a new or existing keyword list or group.

- **List** — a permanent database record you can access any time
- **Group** — a temporary list that expires when you end your current session. Use groups to hold keywords while filtering, then save the keepers to a list before finishing.

### Export
Exports your keyword data in Excel or CSV format. You can export a selection or the entire table.

### Pin Up
When switched on, freezes the filter bar so you can scroll through keyword results while still seeing the column headings.

### Columns Icon
Lets you select which metrics to display in your keyword data table. The default table includes Volume, CPC, Word Count, and PPC Competition. You can also toggle the Keyword Trending filter on or off here.

### Filter Config
Saves your current filter settings as a reusable template for future searches.

- Click **Create New Filter** to save current settings
- Click **Save Changes** to update an existing template
