# 2.5 Golden Filter and Golden Score in Keyword Explorer

## Overview

This lesson covers everything you need to know about the Golden Filter and the Golden Score calculation in WriterZen.

## Background: The Keyword Golden Ratio (KGR)

The Golden Filter is based on the Keyword Golden Ratio (KGR) — a formula created by Doug Cunnington. KGR is a data-driven method for finding keywords with high search demand but low competition, meaning keywords with great potential to rank.

- **Competition** is measured using the All-in-Title operator
- **Demand** is measured by search volume

WriterZen took the KGR concept and built an advanced version called the **Golden Score**. This automates the manual work required by the original KGR formula and removes the 250 monthly volume maximum that limits standard KGR — the Golden Filter works for any search volume.

## Golden Score Formula

```
Golden Score = (All-in-Title ÷ Search Volume) × Class Ratio
```

**Class Ratio** classifies keywords into three levels:

| Level | Description | All-in-Title | Search Volume | Golden Score Range |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | High value — low competition, high volume | < 100 | > 100 | 0 – 30 |
| **Level 2** | Medium value — low competition, low volume | < 100 | < 100 | 30 – 70 |
| **Level 3** | Low value — high competition | > 100 | Any | 70 – 100 |

> **Key principle:** The lower the Golden Score, the greater the chances of that keyword ranking. WriterZen does not recommend going above a Golden Score of **1.618** — a reference to the universal golden ratio, which is why it is called the Golden Score.

## How to Activate the Golden Filter

1. Open your keyword list in Keyword Explorer
2. Click the Golden Filter button
3. Confirm — the number of keyword credits required equals the number of keywords in your list
4. Wait for WriterZen to return the All-in-Title and Golden Score data (no manual work required)
5. You will be notified via in-app notification and email when the process is complete

Once complete, two new columns appear in your results:
- **All-in-Title score**
- **Golden Score**

Sort either column using the up/down arrows. Use the Golden Filter button to adjust the Golden Score range according to your goal.

## Filtering Methods

### Method 1: By Ranking Probability
Best for: **low authority websites**

This method returns keywords with a low All-in-Title score. These keywords have lower search volume but fewer direct competitors — making them easier to rank for. Also known as "low-hanging fruit."

**Example settings:**
- Golden Score: set to 10
- The results panel shows total keywords, combined search volume, average CPC, and average Golden Score that meet the setting
- Click Apply, then sort by All-in-Title under Volume to see your ranked list

### Method 2: By Potential Value
Best for: **established websites with authority and experienced SEO teams**

This method returns keywords with higher search volume but also a higher All-in-Title score. These are more competitive but can bring massive traffic. Suitable for teams willing to invest in a long-term strategy.

**Example settings:**
- Switch to the By Potential Value tab
- The slider is pre-set to the highest recommended Golden Score to capture as many commercially valuable keywords as possible while keeping competition within reason
- Click Apply to filter your list

## Combining Filters for Better Results

You can combine the Golden Filter with other filters to fine-tune your results. Examples:

- Set Golden Filter + All-in-Title max of **10** → keywords with the least direct competition
- Set Search Volume minimum to **100** → keywords with decent monthly traffic worth targeting
