# 4.4 Content Creator: Google NLP Integration

## Overview

This lesson covers WriterZen's integration with the Google Natural Language Processing (NLP) API — what it is, why it matters for SEO, and how to use it inside the Content Creator tool.

## What Is Google NLP?

Google NLP API is a service Google opened to the public around 2018. For the first time, it allows us to see exactly what Googlebot sees when it reads through a piece of content — specifically the **entities** and **sentiment** it detects.

## Brief History: How Google's Understanding Evolved

| Period | How Google Worked |
|---|---|
| **Pre-2012** | Treated keywords as raw strings — ranked pages that contained exact keyword matches, no context or meaning |
| **2012–2013** | Hummingbird update introduced the **Knowledge Graph** — Google began understanding that words have meaning and relationships (e.g. "Steve Jobs" → Tim Cook, Apple, MacBook, Jony Ive) |
| **2018** | BERT introduced — transformed Google's ability to understand context and meaning behind content. Keyword stuffing and low-quality link building stopped working |
| **Now** | Google matches demand (user search) with supply (your content) by understanding entities, context, sentiment, and structure |

> **Google's core job:** Match user demand (search keywords) with the best corresponding supply (your content). The smarter Google gets, the more your content needs to communicate clearly — not just contain the right words.

## What Google NLP Provides

### 1. Entities
Entities are the people, places, things, and concepts that Google associates with your content topic — the building blocks of Google's Knowledge Graph.

**Example:** Writing about "nutrition plan" → Google expects to see entities like meal plan, protein, eating habits, fat, diet plan, intermittent fasting.

When Googlebot scans your content and finds these expected entities, it can immediately understand what your content is about and match it to the right search queries — faster and more accurately.

### 2. Sentiment
Google NLP analyses the overall emotional tone of your content — positive, negative, or neutral.

By seeing the dominant sentiment of your top 20 competitors, you can align your writing tone accordingly. Going against the prevailing sentiment for a topic can confuse Googlebot and hurt your ranking potential.

> **Example:** If top competitors for "nutrition plan" all carry a neutral sentiment, writing in an overly negative or promotional tone may signal a mismatch to Google.

## How to Enable Google NLP in WriterZen

1. In the Project Management Dashboard, click **Add Article**
2. Toggle the **Google NLP** switch to ON
3. Cost: **3 NLP credits per article** — credits are purchased separately from the WriterZen pricing page (one-time purchase)

> **Language support:** Google NLP API currently supports English, Spanish, and Japanese only. The toggle will be disabled for unsupported languages.

## Where Google NLP Appears in the Workflow

### Step 1: Outline to Use
A new **Sentiment column** appears in the competitor list — showing the sentiment Googlebot detected in each competitor's content. Use this to calibrate the tone of your own writing.

### Step 2: Keywords to Include
A **third tab** appears alongside Competitive Keywords and Opportunity Keywords:

**Google NLP Entities tab** — shows every entity extracted from the top 20 competitor websites by the Google NLP bot.

For each entity you can see:
- **General usage** — how often it appears across competitors
- **Suggested usage count** — how many times WriterZen recommends including it in your content
- **Salience score** — how important this entity is to the overall content topic. Higher salience = more critical for Googlebot to understand your content

**Two views available:**

| View | What It Shows |
|---|---|
| **Total Entities** | Complete list of all entities extracted from all top 20 competitors |
| **SERP View** | Browse entity lists per individual competitor — useful for studying how top-ranking sites like Healthline structure their entity usage |

### How to Select Entities

- Click any entity to see how it was used in competitor content
- Add entities that support the outline and theme you set in Step 1
- Do NOT add entities just because they appear in the list — they must fit the content you are writing

> **Rule:** Entities → Keywords → Outline must all tell the same story and support the same theme.

## The Full Content Optimisation Picture

When Googlebot crawls your content, it checks:

1. **Structure** — clear headings, well-organised sections
2. **Keywords** — target keyword and supporting terms present
3. **Entities** — relevant knowledge graph entities included
4. **Internal & external links** — showing you reference and connect to related information
5. **Sentiment** — tone aligned with what Google expects for this topic

Getting all five right is what earns and sustains rankings. Google NLP gives you visibility into points 3 and 5 — which most SEO tools do not provide.

## Key Takeaway

The time you invest in planning — outline, keywords, and entities — directly determines how easily Googlebot can understand and rank your content. A well-structured article with the right entities is not just easier for Googlebot to process — it is also more valuable and coherent for the reader.
