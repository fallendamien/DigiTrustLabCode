# 4.0 Content Creator: Overview

## Overview

This video is an introduction to the Content Creator tool — covering the layout, structure, and core functions to help you build content outlines effectively. Presented by Daniel, CEO and co-founder of WriterZen.

## Accessing the Tool

Access Content Creator directly from the main menu bar at the top of the screen.

## Project Structure

Content Creator organises your work into **projects**. Each project represents a topic cluster or a group of related articles for a specific website.

**Example structure:**
- Website A → Project 1 (main cluster) → list of articles
- Website A → Project 2 (different topic cluster) → list of articles

To start, open an existing project or create a new one, then add a new article from inside the project.

## Article Settings

When creating a new article, you configure:

- **Seed keyword** — the keyword you want to target, typically taken from your keyword cluster results in the previous workflow steps
- **Google NLP** (optional) — when enabled, WriterZen uses Google's Natural Language Processing service to detect sentiment and entities that Google associates with your topic. Including these in your content helps Googlebot understand the context and meaning of your content faster. Can be purchased from the WriterZen pricing page.

## Three-Step Content Creation Workflow

Each article in Content Creator is broken into three sequential steps:

### Step 1: Outline to Use
Focus on building the perfect outline for your content. All design and functions in this step are oriented toward helping you construct a strong content structure before you start writing.

### Step 2: Keywords to Include
Select NLP terms and entities to include in your content — drawn from:
- Terms your competitors are targeting
- Entities returned by Google NLP

As you write, you can check off these terms to ensure full coverage.

### Step 3: Content Writing
Execute the content based on your planned outline and keyword list. This is where the actual writing happens.

## Supporting Features

### Textbook Mode
Enables tooltips and definitions for every term visible on screen. Useful when first learning the tool — turn it off once you are familiar with the interface.

### Tutorial Library
A built-in library of how-to guides, key feature explanations, and related materials accessible directly from within the tool.
