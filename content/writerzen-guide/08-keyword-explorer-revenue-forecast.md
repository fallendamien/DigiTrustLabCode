# 2.6 Revenue Forecast Filter in Keyword Explorer

## Overview

This lesson covers the Revenue Forecast feature — a tool that estimates the number of sales you could make if you were ranking at the top of Google for a specific keyword.

Choosing keywords based on metrics like search volume, keyword difficulty, and CPC alone does not guarantee profitability. Revenue Forecast solves this by translating keyword positions into projected revenue before you invest in a campaign.

> **Important:** Revenue Forecast is only suitable for keywords with **transactional intent** — keywords that lead to a sale. It is not ideal for informational intent keywords, as their conversion rates are typically much lower.

## How It Works: The ESKP Formula

The core metric is **ESKP (Estimated Sales Per Keyword Position)**:

```
ESKP = Search Volume × Click-Through Rate (CTR) × Conversion Rate (CR)
```

| Variable | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Search Volume** | Average monthly search volume on Google for the keyword |
| **CTR** | Percentage of users who click on the result at a given position |
| **CR (Conversion Rate)** | Average number of sales made per 100 visits |

## How to Set Up a Revenue Forecast

1. Click the **Forecast Filter** button to activate it
2. Review the **CTR values** shown across positions: Top 1, Top 3, Top 5, and Top 10 — this data comes from Google and varies by device, location, and product category
3. Use the **CTR Tracker from Advanced Web Ranking** (advancedwebranking.com) to estimate accurate CTR numbers for your specific category and location
4. Set your **target keyword position** (e.g. Top 5)
5. Fill in:
   - **Conversion Rate** — percentage of visitors who make a purchase
   - **Revenue per Conversion** — average order value
   - **Expected Revenue** — your monthly revenue goal
6. WriterZen calculates the forecast automatically

> **Tip:** The easiest way to find your real conversion rate is by running a Google Ads campaign for your target keyword and measuring how many sales occur per 100 visitors.

## ESKP Report Analysis

The ESKP report updates in real time based on your inputs:

| Metric | What It Shows |
|---|---|
| **Estimated Traffic** | Projected visitors based on your CTR at the target position |
| **Estimated Sales** | Projected sales based on estimated traffic × conversion rate |
| **Estimated Revenue** | Projected income based on average revenue per conversion |
| **Goal Difference** | Estimated income minus your sales target (positive = on track) |

## Use Case: Golf Club Sets Business

**Goal:** Earn USD 250,000/month from organic traffic

**Setup:**
- Keyword search: "golf club sets"
- Golden Filter applied first to narrow to ~1,800 keywords with the most ranking potential
- CTR data sourced from Advanced Web Ranking (US, all devices, Sports category)

| Position | CTR | Est. Sets Sold | Est. Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| Top 1 | 24.89% | ~1,997 sets | ~USD 988,500 |
| Top 3 | 9.79% | ~786 sets | ~USD 393,000 |
| Top 5 | 4.90% | ~394 sets | ~USD 197,000 |
| Top 10 | 1.31% | ~105 sets | ~USD 52,500 |

**Inputs used:**
- Target position: Top 5
- Conversion rate: 5%
- Revenue per conversion: USD 500
- Revenue goal: USD 250,000/month

**Conclusion:** At Top 5, the keyword list generates ~USD 197,000 — short of the USD 250,000 goal. This leaves two clear options:

1. Invest in an SEO or Google Ads campaign targeting **Top 3** rankings
2. Target **Top 5** and expand the keyword list until the revenue gap is closed

## Key Takeaway

Revenue Forecast translates keyword positions into a clear ROI picture — in terms of time, money, and effort — before you commit to a campaign. Once you know your projected profit, subtract your campaign costs (use Google Ad Planner for paid campaigns) to calculate your bottom line and decide if the keyword list is worth pursuing.

Play with the Revenue Forecast filter to see how your own campaign numbers stack up.
