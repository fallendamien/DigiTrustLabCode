# 4.2 Content Creator: Keywords to Include

## Overview

This lesson covers Step 2 of the three-step content creation workflow — selecting the right keywords to include in your article. These keywords help communicate your content's context to Googlebot while supporting the outline you built in Step 1.

## Why This Step Matters

WriterZen scans the content of your top 20 competitors and identifies:
1. Keywords they focus on and have used heavily in their content
2. Whether those keywords have real search volume in Google's keyword database

> **Important:** Keywords with no search volume (e.g. filler words like "the," "and," "not") are filtered out — Google is smart enough to ignore these. Only keywords representing actual user demand are surfaced.

## Two Types of Keywords

### 1. Competitive Keywords
Keywords extracted from the content of your top 20 competitors using WriterZen's NLP algorithm. For each keyword you can see:

- **Total usage** — how many times competitors used it across all 20 articles
- **Heading usage** — how many times it appeared in headings specifically
- **Search volume** — Google search demand for that keyword
- **Relevance** — how closely related it is to your seed keyword

Click any keyword to expand it and see exactly where and how competitors used it in their content (highlighted in context).

### 2. Opportunity Keywords
Keywords found in Google's keyword database that your competitors have **not yet used** in their content — representing gaps you can exploit.

These are closely relevant to your seed keyword but currently underutilised by the competition. Adding them gives you an edge.

## How to Use the Keywords to Include Panel

### Interface Layout

| Panel | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Left panel** | Keyword research — browse competitive and opportunity keywords |
| **Right panel** | Your final keyword list — keywords selected for inclusion in the article |

### Adding Keywords

1. Browse the **Competitive Keywords** tab or **Opportunity Keywords** tab
2. Click any keyword to preview how competitors used it
3. Click **Add** to move it to your inclusion list
4. Set the **usage count** — how many times you want that keyword to appear in your article
   - Click the **Edit** button on any keyword in the right panel to adjust usage count
   - WriterZen suggests a default count but you can override it

> **Tip from Daniel:** Don't aim for 40–45 usages. Around 10 is a reasonable target. The goal is natural inclusion, not keyword stuffing.

### Importing Keywords from a List

Click **Import from List** (top right corner) to pull in keywords from a saved WriterZen keyword list — particularly useful when you've already clustered your keywords and want to include the full cluster in one article to rank for multiple keywords simultaneously.

### Adding Custom Keywords

You can also manually enter your own keyword, set a usage target, and click Add to include it in your list.

### Reset to Default

Click **Reset to Default** at any time to clear your selections and return to WriterZen's original suggested keyword set for that article.

## Critical Principle: Keywords Support Content — They Don't Replace It

> **Keywords are a supporting factor, not the determining factor of ranking.**

The determining factors are:
1. **Content quality** — how valuable and useful the content is to the end user
2. **Outline structure** — clear headings, well-organised sections, proper formatting
3. **Keywords to include** — signals to Googlebot what topics and entities your content covers

Including all the keywords on the list will **not** guarantee ranking. Keyword stuffing rarely works and never works long-term. Only include keywords that:
- Fit naturally with the outline you built in Step 1
- Support what you are actually writing about
- Communicate clearly to both Googlebot and your reader

If a suggested keyword doesn't fit your planned content, delete it. If you find a keyword you want to include but your current outline doesn't support it, go back to Step 1 and adjust your outline first.
