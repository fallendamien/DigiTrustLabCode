# 3.6 Domain Authority Score in Keyword Planner

## Overview

This lesson covers the Domain Authority (DA) Score feature in Keyword Planner — what it is, how to activate it, and how to use it to find ranking opportunities.

## What Is Domain Authority?

Domain Authority (DA) is a metric developed by Moz to forecast a website's potential to rank in the SERPs. It is rated on a scale of **0 to 100** — the higher the score, the greater the likelihood of securing top positions.

Moz calculates DA by analysing various factors including site information and backlinks (inbound links), using a model that continuously learns and adapts over time.

> WriterZen uses Moz's DA data via a third-party provider.

## What DA Enables You to Do

- Detect competitor strategies and gain a bird's eye view of their approach to building trust and credibility
- Plan easy-to-rank keywords based on competitive DA levels
- Develop a winning keyword list for execution with a single click

## How to Activate

Click the **Activate Metrics** button in the bottom taskbar. This consumes a number of keyword credits per domain.

Once activated, DA data appears in two places:
1. **Domain Overview Panel** (inside the Domain Filter) — shows DA score for each competing domain
2. **Keyword Data Table** — adds three new filter columns: DA Score, Weak Spot, and related DA metrics

---

## Domain Overview Panel: Reading DA Scores

With DA activated, the Domain Filter overview now shows each competing domain's DA score alongside their market share and keyword count. This gives you an immediate picture of how authoritative each competitor is.

> **High DA = high trust and credibility.** Sites like Healthline, EatingWell, and MedicalNewsToday have very high DA scores — they have earned Google's trust over time.

---

## Keyword Data Table: New DA Metrics

| Metric | What It Shows |
|---|---|
| **Weak Spot** | Number of URLs in the top 10 SERPs that have a DA score below 30 — indicates how easy a keyword is to rank for |
| **Average DA** | Average domain authority of all domains ranking in the top 10 for that keyword |
| **LDA (Lowest DA)** | The lowest DA score among the top 10 ranking URLs |
| **HDA (Highest DA)** | The highest DA score among the top 10 ranking URLs |

**Example:** A keyword with a Weak Spot of 1 means one low-DA domain (DA < 30) is ranking in the top 10 — a signal that the keyword is relatively easy to compete for.

## Use Cases

### Use Case 1: Reverse Engineer High-DA Competitor Content

Study what proven, high-authority competitors are doing right.

1. Open **Domain Filter → Filter Rules**
2. Add filter: **Include Keywords** → select top competitor → **Ranked Top 5**
3. Apply
4. Review the topics, clusters, and keywords your top competitor ranks in the top 5 for
5. Study this content — it has already proven itself worthy of ranking
6. Implement learnings into your own content strategy

---

### Use Case 2: Find Lower-DA Competitors You Can Beat

Identify lower-authority domains that are capturing traffic — especially if your own DA is similar or higher.

1. Open Domain Filter → set results to **Top 50**
2. Scroll through and identify lower-DA domains pulling in decent traffic
3. Select those domains → click **Add Filter**
4. Set: **Include Keywords** → either domain → **Ranked Top 5**
5. Apply
6. Review the keywords those lower-DA domains rank for — if your DA is higher, you can almost certainly compete for and win these keywords

---

### Use Case 3: Find Low-Competition Keywords Using Weak Spot Filter

Filter for keywords where low-DA domains are already ranking in the top 10 — these are your lowest-hanging fruit.

1. In the keyword data table, apply the **Weak Spot filter**
2. Set minimum Weak Spot to **2** (at least 2 low-DA domains in the top 10)
3. Apply
4. Select all topics and check the bottom left panel to see total keywords and clusters filtered
5. The remaining keywords all have at least 2 weak spots — prioritise these for content creation

> The more weak spots a keyword has, the easier it is to rank for — especially if your DA is higher than the low-DA domains currently occupying those positions.

---

### Use Case 4: Filter Keywords by DA Score

Find all keywords currently being ranked by competitors with a lower DA than yours.

1. Open **Domain Filter → DA Score filter**
2. Set the **maximum DA** to just below your own DA score (e.g. if you are DA 76, set max to 70)
3. Apply
4. Results show all keywords in the top 10 ranked by domains with a lower DA than your site
5. These are keywords you can potentially outrank with well-optimised content

Combine with other filters (search volume, CPC, trending) to further refine your priority keyword list.

---

## Summary: DA Filter Strategies

| Goal | Method |
|---|---|
| Study proven content strategies | Filter top competitor → ranked top 5 |
| Find beatable lower-DA competitors | Filter lower-DA domains → ranked top 5 |
| Find easy-to-rank keywords | Weak Spot filter → minimum 2 |
| Find keywords below your DA level | DA Score filter → max = your DA − 5 |

## Key Takeaway

The DA Score feature turns the Keyword Planner into a competitive intelligence tool. In a few clicks you can identify which keywords are realistically within reach, which competitors you can beat, and which content strategies are already proven to rank. Use DA data alongside search volume, weak spots, and the domain filter to build a prioritised, winnable keyword list.
