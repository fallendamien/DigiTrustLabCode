# 4.5 Content Creator: AI Assistant for Outline Building

## Overview

This lesson covers how to use WriterZen's AI Assistant specifically for Step 1 — building your article outline. The AI playground is designed to help you explore ideas, overcome writer's block, and generate structured outline content faster.

## The Role of AI in Outline Building

The AI assistant is most useful when:
- You are unsure what direction to take your content
- You want to explore different copywriting formats
- You find ideas in competitor content you want to expand
- You have writer's block on titles, headings, or structure

> **Important:** SEO structure comes from studying competitors. Creative ideas and content expansion come from the AI. Use both together.

## Accessing the AI Assistant Panel

Click the **AI Assistant** toggle at the bottom of the screen. A new panel opens next to your outline — this is the **Playground**.

The interface has two areas:
- **Right side** — your outline (the result of your research)
- **AI Playground** — where you explore, generate, and experiment with ideas before adding them to the outline

## The Playground: Template Categories

WriterZen has 60+ AI templates. The outline builder surfaces ~20+ templates most relevant to outline creation, organised into two main groups:

### Group 1: Outlines
Templates focused on generating and structuring outline content. Broken into four sub-types:

**1. Orienting By**
Use before you start writing to set the direction and copywriting formula for your content.

| Template | Purpose |
|---|---|
| AIDA | Attention, Interest, Desire, Action |
| Before-After-Bridge | Show problem → solution transformation |
| Problem-Agitate-Solution | Identify pain point, amplify it, offer solution |

**2. Topic To**
Takes a topic, heading, or idea and transforms it into a different format.

| Template | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Topic → Questions | Generate a list of questions from a general topic |
| Topic → Bullet Point Answers | Turn questions into structured answers |
| Topic → Ideas | Expand a topic into multiple content angle ideas |
| Topic → Analogy | Create analogies to explain a topic |
| Topic → Outline | Generate a full outline from a topic |
| Topic → Talk Track | Create a conversational flow from a topic |

**3. Paragraph To**
Takes an existing paragraph and transforms it.

| Template | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Paragraph → Questions | Extract questions from a paragraph |
| Paragraph → Topic | Distil the core topic from a paragraph |
| Paragraph → Outline | Convert paragraph content into an outline structure |
| Paragraph → Key Takeaways | Extract the main points from a paragraph |
| Paragraph → Different Take | Reframe the paragraph from a new angle |

**4. Feature/Format Templates**
| Template | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Feature Benefits | List the benefits of a feature or product |
| How-To Steps | Break a process into numbered steps |
| Pros and Cons | Generate balanced comparison points |
| Unique Value Proposition | Craft a UVP statement for a topic |

### Group 2: Rewrite
Templates for rewriting and adapting existing content.

| Template | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Explain to a Child | Simplify complex competitor content for a general audience |
| Text Rewriter | Rewrite competitor content in your own words to avoid plagiarism |

## How to Use a Template

1. Click any template in the Playground — opens the **Action Panel**
2. Enter your **topic/input** in the field
3. Set **Creativity level** — higher = more diverse and varied results
4. Set **Max Variants** — number of output variations (up to 5)
5. Click **Generate**
6. Review the generated ideas:
   - **Delete** ideas you don't want (saved to a deleted folder — recoverable)
   - **Generate More** to get additional variations
   - Click **Add** on anything you like — it inserts into your outline at the highlighted (blue line) position
7. Once added, select items and format them (H2, H3, H4, bullet point) as needed

> **Blue line indicator:** Before clicking Add, hover over the Add button to see the blue line showing exactly where the item will be inserted in your outline.

### Example Workflow
1. Start with **Topic → Questions** using "nutrition plan for athlete" → generates question ideas
2. Select the questions you like, multi-select with Shift, format as bullet points
3. Click the AI button on a question → use **Question → Bullet Point Answers** to expand it
4. Add answers below the question heading
5. Format as H3 with supporting bullet points

## History Tab

All AI-generated outputs are saved automatically in the **History** tab — organised by date (today, yesterday, past 7 days).

- Access past outputs at any time — useful if you close the tool and return the next day
- Deleted items go to the **Delete Folder** inside History — fully recoverable
- Provides complete visibility of everything the AI generated during your session

## SERP View (3-Panel Mode)

Click **SERP View** at the bottom of the screen to switch to a 3-panel layout:

| Panel | Content |
|---|---|
| Left | Competitor content (SERP results) |
| Centre | AI Playground / Action Panel |
| Right | Your outline |

This lets you research competitors, generate AI ideas, and build your outline simultaneously without switching between views.

> **Tip for smaller screens:** Click the expand button in the top-right corner of the outline panel to gain ~200 extra pixels of workspace.

In 3-panel mode, click the competitor list button to access all top 20 competitors directly — no need to navigate away.

---

## AI-Generated Title & Description

In the article edit screen, the title and description fields each have an **AI Generate button**:

- **Title generator** — uses the seed keyword and scans all top 20 competitor titles to generate the most relevant title options
- **Description generator** — activates only after a title is set; generates a description based on the confirmed title

---

## Key Takeaway

The AI Playground is designed for exploration — not perfection on the first try. Generate ideas freely, delete what doesn't fit (nothing is permanently gone), and keep building until your outline tells a clear, structured story. The better your outline, the better your AI-assisted writing will be in Step 3.
